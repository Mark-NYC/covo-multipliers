The gap

Most young leaders are trained for jobs, not ownership.

You can learn a trade, finish a business degree, or pick up a practical skill and still not know how to build something real.

You still need reps, customers, decisions, pressure, and coaching from people who have actually built.

01

Business school often stops too soon.

Concepts are helpful. But a young leader still needs real customer reps, ownership pressure, and wise coaching.

02

Ministry training often floats above real life.

Disciple-making has to move into work, customers, neighborhoods, teams, and daily relational access.

03

Income is treated like a side issue.

Calling without a workable income model eventually becomes stress, dependency, or drift.

04

Mission gets separated from work.

The result is a split life: church over here, job over there, and mission somewhere else.

What is a sentrepreneur?

A sent entrepreneur.

A sentrepreneur builds profitable work as a platform for disciple-making, leadership development, and church multiplication.

Not just a Christian business owner.

The goal is not to slap faith language on a company. The goal is to build work that creates real value, forms leaders, opens relational access, and helps disciples multiply through everyday life.

The pathway

From skill to ownership to multiplication.

The launchpad is staged on purpose. Leaders learn, practice, build, test ownership, and grow into greater responsibility over time.

01

Foundation

Clarify your field, your business lane, your mission posture, and your first offer.

02

Validate

Talk to real customers, find painful problems, and learn what people will actually pay for.

03

Build

Create a simple offer, delivery system, follow-up rhythm, and clear next step.

04

Launch

Get first customers, deliver real value, track numbers, and build confidence through reps.

05

Multiply

Reinvest, train others, build systems, and turn the work into a platform for mission.

What you learn

This is skills training, not vague inspiration.

The curriculum is built around doing. Short teaching. Real field work. Coaching. Clear next reps.

Money and Ownership

Mission Rhythms

Leadership Multiplication
