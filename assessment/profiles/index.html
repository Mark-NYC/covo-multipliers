Reference Guide

Fivefold Stewardship Profiles

Stewardship patterns, not identity labels.

These profiles describe ways disciples may contribute to the body of Christ. They do not determine calling, office, maturity, or readiness. Use them for reflection, team discernment, coaching, and shared language.

Seven Stewardship Patterns

Each pattern describes a recurring orientation — how someone tends to notice, contribute, and operate. No profile is superior. Most people carry more than one, with one or two that are more consistent.

Prophetic Stewardship

A pattern of noticing spiritual significance, drift, inconsistency, or needed correction, then helping people or communities return to truth and faithfulness.

Tends to noticeDrift, inconsistency, compromise, hidden issues, misalignment.
Healthy contributionTruth, discernment, courage, biblical faithfulness.
Common shadowCriticism, harshness, certainty without testing.

Evangelistic Stewardship

A pattern of connecting naturally with people far from Jesus, communicating the gospel clearly, and helping others move toward faith and belonging.

Tends to noticeOutsiders, openness, relational access, gospel opportunities.
Healthy contributionGospel clarity, invitation, relational connection.
Common shadowPressure, shallow follow-up, counting conversations without formation.

Shepherding Stewardship

A pattern of caring for people, protecting relational health, staying present through difficulty, and helping people experience safety, belonging, and restoration.

Tends to noticeHurting people, neglected people, relational breakdown, loneliness.
Healthy contributionCare, protection, belonging, restoration.
Common shadowConflict avoidance, rescuing, unhealthy dependence.

Teaching Stewardship

A pattern of helping people understand Scripture, truth, and the reasons behind faithful practice.

Tends to noticeConfusion, assumptions, weak foundations, misunderstanding.
Healthy contributionClarity, biblical grounding, understanding, obedience.
Common shadowOver-explaining, information control, expert dependence.

Apostolic Direction

A pattern of seeing new kingdom possibilities, moving toward people or places with limited gospel access, taking responsible initiative, and helping others move into action.

Tends to noticeNew opportunities, unreached people, strategic gaps, barriers, next steps.
Healthy contributionInitiative, strategy, boundary-crossing, mobilization.
Common shadowAmbition, restlessness, novelty-seeking, unfinished starts.

Apostolic Formation

A pattern of grounding mission in prayer, Scripture, obedience, endurance, communal discernment, and tested dependence on God.

Tends to noticePrayer deficits, shallow roots, unhealthy motives, spiritual drift.
Healthy contributionDependence, depth, endurance, discernment.
Common shadowOver-spiritualized delay, private certainty, passivity.

Apostolic Multiplying

A pattern of developing disciples and leaders, transferring convictions and responsibility, sharing authority, and helping work continue beyond the original catalyst.

Tends to noticeBottlenecks, leadership gaps, dependency, succession problems.
Healthy contributionEntrustment, leader development, release, local ownership.
Common shadowDelegation without authority, control, premature release.

What Happens When Each Pattern Is Missing

Teams and communities tend to drift in predictable ways when a stewardship pattern is absent, underrepresented, or silent.

Without Prophetic

Drift goes unnamed, compromise grows, and hard truths remain unsaid.

Without Evangelistic

The community turns inward and fewer people far from Jesus are engaged.

Without Shepherding

People fall through cracks, burnout rises, and belonging weakens.

Without Teaching

Truth becomes vague, practices lose grounding, and confusion spreads.

Without Apostolic Direction

Good intentions stall, opportunities are missed, and movement stays reactive.

Without Apostolic Formation

Activity outruns dependence, prayer weakens, and character lags behind influence.

Without Apostolic Multiplying

Leaders are not developed, work bottlenecks, and growth depends on key people.

Two-Profile Combination Directory

When two patterns are both consistently present, they tend to shape a distinctive contribution and a distinctive risk. These are patterns to observe — not fixed types.

Prophetic + Evangelistic

ContributionBrings truth and gospel clarity into relationships with people far from Jesus.

RiskUrgency can outrun trust, listening, or patient follow-up.

Prophetic + Shepherding

ContributionPerceives what is happening beneath the surface and stays present with people through it.

RiskCan carry other people's burdens too deeply or feel responsible to fix what was only meant to be named.

Prophetic + Teaching

ContributionRecognizes drift or error and explains the biblical truth behind it.

RiskCan become hard to question when discernment and teaching authority reinforce each other.

Prophetic + Apostolic Direction

ContributionSees where change is needed and moves first toward correction, reform, or new kingdom opportunity.

RiskCan act too quickly on an untested impression.

Prophetic + Apostolic Formation

ContributionCombines spiritual perception with prayer, Scripture, endurance, and communal testing.

RiskCan become overly inward or treat private spiritual intensity as proof of accuracy.

Prophetic + Apostolic Multiplying

ContributionNames core convictions clearly and helps preserve truth as responsibility passes to others.

RiskCan over-control transmission or mistake uniformity for faithfulness.

Evangelistic + Shepherding

ContributionBuilds trust with people far from Jesus and stays with them beyond the first conversation.

RiskCan soften necessary truth to preserve connection.

Evangelistic + Teaching

ContributionConnects naturally with outsiders and explains the gospel and Scripture in understandable ways.

RiskCan turn relationships into teaching projects or overload people with information.

Evangelistic + Apostolic Direction

ContributionMoves toward people and places with limited gospel access and creates meaningful contact.

RiskCan move fast without enough listening, cultural learning, or sustained presence.

Evangelistic + Apostolic Formation

ContributionJoins outward gospel engagement with prayer, dependence, endurance, and tested obedience.

RiskCan allow preparation or discernment to delay actual engagement.

Evangelistic + Apostolic Multiplying

ContributionConnects with people far from Jesus while helping new disciples share through their own relationships.

RiskCan push rapid multiplication before people are grounded and formed.

Shepherding + Teaching

ContributionCreates relational safety while helping people understand and obey Scripture.

RiskCan become overly protective, over-explaining, or dependency-producing.

Shepherding + Apostolic Direction

ContributionMoves toward new people or places while paying close attention to the wellbeing of those involved.

RiskCan slow necessary movement to avoid discomfort or conflict.

Shepherding + Apostolic Formation

ContributionBuilds communities marked by care, spiritual depth, accountability, and endurance.

RiskCan become inward, cautious, or overly focused on stability.

Shepherding + Apostolic Multiplying

ContributionDevelops people with patience and care, giving responsibility without abandoning them.

RiskCan hold people too closely and delay real release.

Teaching + Apostolic Direction

ContributionBrings biblical clarity and strategic thinking into new or underdeveloped contexts.

RiskCan design the work before listening deeply to the context.

Teaching + Apostolic Formation

ContributionGrounds mission in Scripture, theological clarity, prayer, disciplined obedience, and endurance.

RiskCan allow preparation, precision, or caution to delay action.

Teaching + Apostolic Multiplying

ContributionExplains truth clearly and helps others teach, correct, and transmit it faithfully.

RiskCan control reproduction through preferred language, methods, or theological systems.

Apostolic Direction + Apostolic Formation

ContributionCombines movement toward new fields with prayer, tested obedience, accountability, and endurance.

RiskCan become imbalanced toward action without depth or formation without movement.

Apostolic Direction + Apostolic Multiplying

ContributionCombines pioneering initiative with developing people who can carry the work forward.

RiskCan start and delegate quickly without enough formation, testing, or care.

Apostolic Formation + Apostolic Multiplying

ContributionCombines spiritual depth, endurance, and accountability with intentional disciple and leader development.

RiskCan delay release until people meet an unrealistic standard of readiness.

How to Use This With a Team

These questions work well as a facilitated conversation after a team has reviewed their profile patterns together. No shared language replaces honest, specific observation — but shared language can make the conversation easier to start.

What patterns are strongest on our team?

What pattern is missing or consistently quiet?

Which strength may be over-functioning — carrying more than it should?

Where do we need another person, not just more effort from the people we have?

Who needs permission to contribute differently than they have been?

Where are we confusing a stewardship pattern with maturity or readiness?

Where are we depending on one person instead of investing in developing others?
