How are you wired to serve?

The Fivefold Stewardship Assessment explores how you tend to see, engage, and contribute in ministry — across the five patterns from Ephesians 4: Apostolic, Prophetic, Evangelistic, Shepherding, and Teaching.

This takes about 20–25 minutes. Your progress is saved automatically so you can take a break and return.

This is a pilot assessment. It has not yet been tested enough to show how consistent its scores are or how well they reflect the patterns it aims to measure. Use these results as one starting point for reflection and conversation — not to make a final decision about your calling or identity.

What to expect

You'll encounter several types of questions:

  • Agreement statements — how much a statement sounds like you
  • Frequency questions — how often you do something
  • Example questions — whether you can point to a real past experience
  • Scenario questions — which response feels most natural to you

Respond based on how you actually are, not how you think you should be. There are no right or wrong answers.

A note on outcome questions

Some questions ask what you have seen happen in others as a result of your involvement. These questions do not assume your contribution was the primary cause of anything. They explore whether you have had experience in certain areas. Answer honestly based on what you have observed — including "I haven't seen this" where that is true.
