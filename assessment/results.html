How to Use These Results

Methodology and Limitations

This is a pilot assessment. It has not yet been tested enough to show how consistent its scores are or how well they reflect the patterns it aims to measure. The score bands (Strong, Moderate-high, and so on) are working estimates. They are not established categories.

This assessment measures what you said about yourself — not what others would observe. A high score shows how you answered. It does not prove that people who know you would see the same thing.

Self-report assessments have real limits. You may answer the way you wish you were, or the way you think a good answer sounds. You may not see your own blind spots. The caution indicators can catch some patterns, but they cannot catch everything.

Do not use these results alone to make ministry placement decisions, assign leadership roles, or draw conclusions about calling, gifting, or spiritual maturity. These results are a starting point for reflection and honest conversation. Use them as one part of a deeper discernment process.

What a Deeper Assessment Process Would Add

Answering questions about yourself is a useful starting point. But it cannot verify your patterns the way a fuller process can. A deeper assessment would typically add:

  • Feedback from people who have seen you in ministry — not just your own view
  • Specific examples with real dates and outcomes, confirmed by others
  • A look at character and faithfulness in daily life, not only pattern scores
  • Group discernment with a sending or accountability community
  • A review of your context, not just your individual tendencies
  • Evidence of actual fruit — sustained investment, real relationships, impact over time
  • Patterns that have lasted for years and been passed on to others

If Covo Multipliers offers a deeper process in the future, it would be described here. For now, these results are a tool for reflection, not a full assessment.
