How to Use These Results

Methodology and Limitations

This is an unvalidated pilot instrument. The Fivefold Stewardship Assessment has not yet been subjected to psychometric validation. Scores are provisional and subject to revision after analysis of pilot data. Band thresholds (Strong, Moderate-high, etc.) are working estimates, not clinically or theologically validated cutoffs.

The assessment measures self-reported perception, behavioral recall, and scenario judgment — not observed behavior, character, or stewardship fruitfulness. A high score reflects what you reported about yourself, not what others would confirm.

Self-report instruments are subject to social desirability bias, limited self-awareness, and the framing effects of specific items. Shadow indicators may detect some patterns but cannot comprehensively detect all blind spots.

These results are best used as a structured starting point for reflection and conversation. They are not a basis for ministry placement, leadership decisions, or theological conclusions about calling, gifting, or spiritual maturity.

What a Deeper Assessment Process Would Add

A self-report instrument like this one is a useful starting point, but it cannot verify stewardship patterns the way a more comprehensive process can. A deeper assessment would typically add:

  • Observer or peer feedback from people who have seen you in ministry contexts
  • Verified behavioral examples with specific dates and outcomes
  • Character and local-faithfulness review, not only domain-pattern scoring
  • Communal discernment within a sending or accountability community
  • Review of calling and sending context, not only individual orientation
  • Evidence of actual stewardship — sustained investment, relational depth, community impact
  • Patterns that have endured over time and been reproduced in others

If Covo Multipliers offers a deeper process in the future, it would be described here. For now, these results are offered as a reflective tool, not a comprehensive assessment.
