Before you begin

Read these instructions once before starting. They apply throughout the assessment.

Respond as you actually are

Answer based on your genuine patterns — not how you think a mature Christian should answer, not how you wish you were, and not based on a particularly good or difficult season you've had. Think about how you characteristically operate over the last year or two.

Question types you'll encounter

Agreement questions

A statement is presented. You choose how much it describes you, from Strongly disagree to Strongly agree.

Frequency questions

You're asked how often something happened over the past 12 months. Choose the option that most accurately reflects your actual frequency.

Example questions

You're asked whether you can point to a real past experience. The scale runs from Cannot identify any example to Others could readily confirm this. Only choose a higher option if you can point to a specific, real example.

Scenario questions

A situation is described. Four responses are offered. Choose the one that feels most natural to you — not the one that seems most spiritually mature. There is no correct answer.

Forced-choice questions

You'll be asked to choose between two or three options that both seem reasonable. Pick whichever feels more like you, even if neither is a perfect fit.

A note on outcome and impact questions

Some questions ask what you have seen happen in others as a result of your ministry involvement. These questions do not assume you were the cause of what happened. They explore whether you have had experience in certain areas of ministry.

Answer based on what you have genuinely observed — including answering honestly when you have not seen something.

Saving your progress

Your answers are saved automatically as you go. If you need to stop and return later, use the Save & Exit button. You'll receive a link to your email address to resume where you left off.
