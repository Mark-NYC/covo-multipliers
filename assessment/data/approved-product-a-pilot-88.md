### A3. Three Scenario Rewrites



**PP-009 — Revised Prophetic Scenario**



*Problem with previous version:* Option A ("return to the relevant Scripture passages to see what they actually say") was the obviously Prophetic-correct and Teaching-correct response simultaneously. Any scripturally conscientious respondent would choose it regardless of function.



*Revised scenario:* Places a community practice under uncertain scrutiny, requiring the Prophetic response to include provisional naming with humility and community testing — not simply "read the Bible."



*Function signals:* A = TE-1 (careful textual study before acting); B = SH-1 (pastoral attention to affected people); C = PL-3 strongest Prophetic signal (timely provisional naming with accountability); D = cautious observation (wisdom or avoidance — both plausible)



*Primary tag corrected:* PL-3 (was PL-1). PL-1 remains secondary (Scripture-return is option A, not the primary Prophetic signal).



---



**PT-009 — Revised Teaching Scenario**



*Problem with previous version:* Options A+B together were obviously the mature pair; option C (address it in the group without preparation) read as clearly inferior; this tested generic wisdom rather than function.



*Revised scenario:* Uses a teaching that misses what the text means but is not dangerously heretical, presenting four genuinely reasonable responses that distinguish Teaching instinct (careful study + private dialogue) from Prophetic (immediate naming) from Shepherding-adjacent (wait until it causes confusion).



*Function signals:* A = TE-1 (study the text carefully first); B = TE-3 (private dialogue, relational correction); C = TE-2/communal (invite group examination of text); D = pastoral caution (wait for evidence of harm — not necessarily wrong)



---



**PAD-008 — Revised Apostolic Direction Scenario**



*Problem with previous version:* Option B ("begin something small and invite others into what you've started") read as the obviously Apostolic-correct response; waiting options felt passive or immature; the scenario tested generic action-versus-caution rather than Apostolic function specifically.



*Revised scenario:* Adds information-incompleteness and frames the Apostolic signal as a small *reversible* experiment with *invitation* — proportionate initiative rather than immediate unilateral action. Other options represent genuine maturity patterns, not failures.



*Function signals:* A = FI-1 Apostolic (small reversible experiment with invitation — proportionate, learning-oriented); B = FI-3/wisdom (deepen contextual understanding first); C = EC-1/relational (route through existing leader; institutional or collaborative instinct); D = consensus-building (wait for committed partner; SH-4 or community-readiness instinct)

## SECTION F: READINESS STATEMENT



**Conclusion: Ready for expert review and cognitive interviews, with two minor flagged concerns.**



The revised 88-item pilot bank corrects all known structural deficiencies from the previous version: all six thin constructs now have at least one behavioral item, three problematic scenarios have been rewritten to present genuine four-way tensions, format mismatches have been resolved, disclaimers have been relocated to a block instruction, and all audit tables reconcile exactly at 88 items.



**Two concerns that do not block expert review but should be addressed before cognitive interviews begin:**



First, FI-3 (Boundary-Crossing Adaptability) and LE-3 (Developing and Recognizing Leaders) have zero items in the pilot bank. Adding one item each from the deferred pool — AD-009 for FI-3, AM-017 for LE-3 — before cognitive interviews would bring the bank to 90 items and eliminate two absent-construct flags. This is optional but recommended.



Second, three scenarios (PP-009, PT-009, PAD-008) have been substantially rewritten and have not yet been tested with practitioners. A rapid practitioner check of these three scenarios — confirming that all four options read as plausible rather than obviously ranked — should precede full cognitive interviews to avoid wasting cognitive-interview sessions on items that need basic wording revision.



Estimated pilot completion time: approximately 20–30 minutes, to be confirmed through cognitive interviews and timing data. The eventual 45–60-item public form may target 10–15 minutes after final reduction.
