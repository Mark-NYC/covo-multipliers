Sentrepreneur Academy

Learn a skill. Build real work. Make disciples through it.

A skills-based academy for emerging leaders who want practical business training, hands-on coaching, and a pathway to live sent through everyday work.

Join the Interest List See the Pathway

Built for young builders, tradesmen, service business starters, marketplace missionaries, and leaders who do not want to split business and mission.

The gap

Most young leaders are trained for jobs, not ownership.

You can learn a trade, finish a business degree, or pick up a practical skill and still not know how to build something real.

You still need to learn how to get customers, manage money, create a simple offer, take ownership, and turn your work into a field for disciple-making.

That is the gap Sentrepreneur Academy is built to close.

Business school often stops too soon.

Concepts are helpful. But a young leader still needs reps, customers, decisions, pressure, and coaching from people who have actually built.

Ministry training often floats above real life.

Disciple-making cannot stay in a classroom. It has to move into work, customers, neighborhoods, teams, and the relationships already forming around daily life.

What is a sentrepreneur?

A sent entrepreneur.

A sentrepreneur builds profitable work as a platform for disciple-making, leadership development, and church multiplication.

Not just a Christian business owner.

The goal is not to slap faith language on a company. The goal is to build work that creates real value, forms leaders, opens relational access, and helps disciples multiply through everyday life.

The pathway

From skill to ownership to multiplication.

The academy is staged on purpose. We are not trying to launch everyone on day one. Leaders learn, practice, build, test ownership, and grow into greater responsibility over time.

01

Learn the foundations

Business basics, disciple-making habits, simple tools, and how to see work as a field.

02

Take real reps

Practice with customers, conversations, rhythms, service, follow-up, and simple weekly action.

03

Build low-overhead work

Start with practical businesses or simple offers that force ownership and customer learning fast.

04

Live sent through the work

Make disciples through the relationships forming around your business, community, and daily rhythms.

05

Return and multiply

Launched builders help train, coach, fund, and encourage the next wave of sent entrepreneurs.

Training tracks

Start with practical work that can actually get moving.

We are especially interested in low-overhead businesses and practical service lanes because they create ownership fast. No fantasy entrepreneurship. Real offers. Real customers. Real responsibility.

Home service businesses

Window washing, lawn care, cleaning, repair, and other simple services that can start lean and teach customer ownership quickly.

Trades and apprenticeships

Practical skill paths like HVAC, electrical, maintenance, and other work that can become a platform for business and mission.

AI and business systems

Helping small businesses improve processes, save time, and build better systems through practical AI implementation.
